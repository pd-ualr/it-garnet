About Crowley's Ridge

Feel free to explore the beauty of Crowley’s Ridge State Park and immerse yourself in its rich history and natural wonders!

What is Crowley’s Ridge State Park?

Crowley’s Ridge State Park is a 291-acre state park located in Greene County, Arkansas, atop the unique geological formation known as Crowley’s Ridge. The park offers a rustic experience with native log and stone structures constructed by the Civilian Conservation Corps (CCC) in the 1930s. Visitors can explore hiking trails, enjoy a 31-acre fishing lake, and relax in a 3.5-acre swimming area.

Where is the park located?

Crowley’s Ridge is a fascinating geological formation believed to have been shaped by water, ice, and wind over a 50-million-year period. It’s an island formed by ancient paths of the Mississippi and Ohio Rivers2.

What makes Crowley’s Ridge unique?
